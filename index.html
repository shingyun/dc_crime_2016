Are you a resident in Washington, D.C.? Do you work or study in D.C.? An analysis of data on crimes in DC in 2016 provides you a glance of crime-prone areas and what kind of crimes you should be aware of in DC.

Theft is the biggest crime in DC

Last year, 13,951 theft crimes occurred in Washington, D.C.. The number of theft crimes in 2016 is a bit lower than 2015 when 14,016 occurred. However, theft is still the top crime in D.C. and mostly happened during daytime in downtown D.C., Capital Hill and surrounding areas.

Theft is the most serious crimes in D.C., followed by auto theft which had 10,789 crimes last year. Theft and auto theft are the only two crimes that occurred above 10,000 times in 2016. Robbery, motor vehicle theft, assault and burglary were between 2,000 to 3,000 times. Sex abuse, homicide were below 350. Arson had 6.

Theft in this analysis refers to “ Theft/Other” in the original data set. According to Metropolitan Police Department of D.C., Theft/Other is a broad inclusion of theft offenses and excludes theft of items from auto(auto theft) and motor vehicle or motor vehicle itself(motor vehicle theft).

Thieves steal in daytime

The time when crimes happened in analysis is estimated to be the earliest possible occurred date and time of the crimes, instead of the report date.

Most of the theft happened in daytime, especially during afternoon and reached the peak at 15:00 when people are still in office or school. The number of theft crimes declined from evening, but was higher than other crimes. Auto left has a similar pattern as theft.

Violent crimes such as robbery and assault inclined slightly from noon to midnight. The numbers of sex abuse and homicide distributed relatively evenly.

Thieves are highly active in downtown DC

The crime map based on nine kinds of offenses in D.C. shows that downtown D.C., Capital Hill and nearby areas suffer from theft and auto left seriously.

Violent crimes such as robbery, assault, sex abuse and homicide are more likely to happen in central and eastern D.C.. Western D.C. is a relatively safe neighborhood.
